Welcome to the Video Timing Calculator

This tool is designed to help you calculate and configure various parameters for video timing and MIPI configurations. Explore the following pages to get started:

Purpose: Provide quick, accurate calculations for video timing (active area, blanking intervals, pixel clock) and MIPI D-PHY parameters based on display presets or custom inputs.

Target audience: FPGA/SoC designers, display interface engineers, firmware developers, applications engineers, and validation/test teams working with MIPI DSI/CSI and general video pipelines.

Typical use cases: sizing blanking for timing closure, estimating lane rates and bandwidth, validating sensor/display configurations, and sharing configurations across teams.

Who should use this: Teams building camera/display interfaces, prototyping embedded vision systems, or validating board-level video paths on Lattice devices.

What it covers:

  • Video timing fundamentals: total/active pixels, horizontal/vertical blanking, frame rate, pixel clock.
  • MIPI D-PHY sizing: lane count, lane rate, payload efficiency, byte/bit clocks.
  • Color formats and bit depths: RGB and YCbCr options with derived bits-per-pixel.

How to use:

  1. Open Video Timing and select a preset resolution or enter custom values.
  2. Adjust horizontal/vertical blanking as needed; derived fields update automatically.
  3. Review outputs (pixel clock, totals) and proceed to Soft D-PHY to size lane rate and lanes.
  4. Use MIPI Configuration to finalize format and interface details.
  5. Optionally export inputs/outputs as JSON for documentation and reviews.

Assumptions & limitations:

  • Calculations assume synchronous pixel domain and typical D-PHY efficiency; verify exact requirements with panel/sensor datasheets.
  • Results provide engineering estimates; hardware validation is recommended.
  • MIPI Configuration: Configure MIPI-related parameters.
  • Soft D-PHY Parameter: Adjust and calculate D-PHY parameters.
  • Video Timing: Calculate video timing parameters.
